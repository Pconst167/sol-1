                        THE OREGON SPLIT: 503 vs. 541

                             Decided: 2 May 1995
                 Permissive dialing begins: 5 November 1995
                    Permissive dialing ends: 30 June 1996
    List updated with respect to Lincoln County, border towns: 2 June 1995

        This is a list of the prefixes that will stay in 503 and those
that will switch to 541, based on the information that I have received
on the TV news, in our local newspaper, and from talking to persons
from Oregon's Public Utilities Commission (PUC). The prefixes which
are listed are those which are currently in use in the great State of
Oregon.

        Basically, all of the northwestern part of Oregon (including
Portland, its suburbs, and Salem) stays in 503, and all the rest
(including the rather populous cities of Eugene, Medford, and Bend)
gets 541. (For those interested, the breakdown of which county goes
where is at the end of the document.)

        My earlier list was in error as to Lincoln County and a few
locations on the border of the split. The news I had heard on 2 May
1995 stated that Lincoln County would retain 503. The news I got from
the PUC spokesperson a few days later said that all of Lincoln County
was going 541 to keep continuity. I have updated my list accordingly,
as well as my county-by-county breakdown at the end of the list.

        Also, thanks to the many people who sent in information as to
where Black Cap was! 503-219 is a cellular prefix for Lakeview, as I
have listed here. If you see any other strange things on this list, or
anything that is incorrect, let me know! Until October or November I
will be E-mailable as "csauls@eosc.osshe.edu" (no quotes). Anyway,
here's the list:

                             BREAKDOWN BY COUNTY

This is pretty much true. There are a couple of exceptions in Linn and
Marion counties where a city went to the other code to preserve local
continuity.

Counties which will stay in 503:

        Clackamas, Clatsop, Columbia, southern Hood River county (503-337 and
        503-622, Mt. Hood Meadows and the surrounding area), Marion (except
        for 541-327, Jefferson), Multnomah, Polk, Tillamook, Washington,
        Yamhill

Counties which will get 541:

        Baker, Benton, Coos, Crook, Curry, Deschutes, Douglas, Gilliam, Grant,
        Harney, northern Hood River county, Jackson, Jefferson, Josephine,
        Klamath, Lake, Lincoln, Linn (except for 503-859, Lyons), Lane,
        Malheur, Morrow, Sherman, Umatilla, Union, Wallowa, Wasco, Wheeler
