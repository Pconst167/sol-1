                   What is Copy-Protection ?

  Copy-protection  is a  scheme  used by  a  software publisher  to
  protect his product from unauthorized  copying.

  Sometimes  the distribution  diskette can  not be  copied by  DOS
  commands.   Sometimes the  number  of times  the  product can  be
  installed to a hard drive  is limited.  Sometimes before starting
  up, the program  asks for information which can  only be answered
  by referring to  the manual or other  materials which accompanied
  the original product.

  This  last category  is  called  paper protection.  It  can  also
  include  devices like  code-wheels,  no-copy  paper with  special
  pictures on it, and colored plastic overlays.


                   What is Wrong with Copy-Protection ?

  Copy-protection,  in attempting  to  prevent unauthorized  people
  from  using  the  product,  can interfere  with  the  secure  and
  pleasurable use of the product by legitimate customers.

  A diskette is a fragile thing.   Each time the copy-protection is
  tested, the head of the  floppyÿdrive makes physical contact with
  the diskette.  If the diskette  becomes unreadable, you will find
  that you are locked out of your own software.

  Some manufacturers allow the copy-protection to be installed to a
  hard drive,  thus avoiding the  diskette friction  problem.  This
  works fine  until you experience a  hard drive crash.   After you
  restore  from   backup  you  discover   that  your   install  has
  disappeared and you are locked out of your own software again.

  Paper based  protection is just  plain annoying.  Having  to hunt
  for the manual or  code wheel every time you play  your game gets
  to be irritating very quickly, especially  if you own many games.
  If that  piece of  paper is  lost, stolen  or destroyed,  you are
  locked out of the software that you paid for.

  You must hope  that the manufacturer of the software  is still in
  business, still is  shipping the product that you  are using, and
  is friendly  enough to ship new  materials for a  reasonable fee.
  And you  still are locked  out of your  own software for  days or
  weeks until you receive the requested materials.

  Then you wait for the whole cycle to repeat.

                   How Do I Use NeverLock?

  Nothing could be simpler.  NeverLock comes as a single executable
  file, with  no accessory files  to keep  track of.  Just  copy it
  from the  distribution diskette to your  hard drive and  then run
  the EXE  file.  Watch the  introduction until the  'Conditions of
  Use' appear.  Answer the question and  the main menu will appear.
  You may hit any key to bypass the animation.

  Naturally, NeverLock itself  is not copy-protected.  You  may use
  the DOS copy command to back it up.

  When  the main  menu  appears  use the  arrow  keys  to move  the
  highlight bar to any option and press the Enter key to select it.
  The program  guides you every step  of the way  with instructions
  and  dialogue  boxes,  and  there is  always  help  available  by
  pressing the F1 key.   There is full mouse support if  you have a
  Microsoft compatible mouse driver loaded.

  NeverLock removes protection  by searching the program  files for
  the data that needs to be  changed, and then making those changes
  with no intervention from you.

  Once  the copy-protection  is removed,  you  do not  need to  use
  NeverLock again on that particular product.

  NeverLock needs 2 megabytes of free  space on your hard drive for
  its work files.  This can be any hard drive on your system.

  At the main  menu, select the product you wish  to unprotect from
  either the 'Modern  Collection' list or the  'Classic Collection'
  list.  NeverLock will then prompt you for the letter of the drive
  your product is installed on.  It  scans this drive and shows you
  a list of all the directories it found.

  Select the directory  where the product is  installed.  NeverLock
  will now display  a confirmation dialogue box  explaining exactly
  what it  thinks you  want it to  do, and giving  you a  chance to
  stop.   If  you decide  to  proceed,  NeverLock will  search  the
  directory you specified for the copy-protected files.

  NeverLock creates a work directory on your hard drive.  The first
  one  will be  called NEV_001,  and subsequent  ones are  numbered
  upward.  It copies the original files  to this work directory and
  then attempts to remove the copy-protection from the new files.

  NeverLock will now  prompt you again, offering  to exchange these
  work files for  the original files in the  product directory.  If
  you agree,  it will  perform the switch  and leave  your original
  files in  its work directory.  In  case the unprotected  files do
  not function properly, the originals are thus preserved.

  In some cases NeverLock creates a special loader file.  Watch its
  report screen carefully.  If necessary, it will tell you that you
  must start  your protected product  with  the special loader file
  instead of the normal product file.

  NeverLock monitors the whole process and presents you with a
  log file that holds:
    The name of the protected product.
    What files were copied, searched, exchanged or moved.
    Where the original files and unprotected files are located.
    A success or failure message.

  Exit NeverLock,  change to  the product  directory and  start the
  product.  If a  quiz screen appears, just enter  any answer.  The
  product will think your answer is correct and continue normally.
